Where Is John Larroquette Going Leaving Night Court On NBC?

John Larroquette, the Actor who played the famous defense attorney Dan Fielding in NBC’s Night Court, has decided to leave the show and seek new job opportunities.

This news has been confirmed in a sneak peek from the May 2 edition of Night Court, in which comedian Pete Holmes performed.

In the clip, Judge Abby Stone, played by Melissa Rauch, informs her fiancé Rand that Dan is relocating and that she wants to throw him a farewell Party.

Despite Abby’s efforts to convince Dan to stay, he has decided to pursue his career path and move on.

Dan and Abby’s close friendship developed after she took over the courtroom that previously belonged to her late Father, Judge Harry Stones. Although Abby is sad to see Dan go, she cannot stop him from following his dreams.

She promises to throw him the most excellent goodbye Party the town has ever seen. The sneak peek ends with Abby jokingly telling Dan that he’s not going to the bayou before they say “bye you.”

It is unclear where John Larroquette is going after leaving Night Court, but fans are excited to see what new opportunities he will pursue.

This departure marks the end of an era for the beloved sitcom, and viewers will surely miss Dan Fielding’s quick wit and charming personality.

John Larroquette in Night Court

John Larroquette’s portrayal of Dan Fielding in the popular NBC sitcom Night Court was a standout performance.

His character, a suave and egotistical prosecutor, provided a hilarious contrast to the chaotic and eccentric happenings in the night court.

Larroquette’s comedic timing and delivery made Fielding one of the most beloved characters on the show.

Larroquette’s work on Night Court was recognized with four consecutive Emmy Awards for Outstanding Supporting Actor in a Comedy Series from 1985 to 1988.

The show ran for nine seasons from 1984 to 1992, and Larroquette appeared in every episode alongside Harry Anderson and Richard Moll.

Although there was talk of a spin-off featuring Fielding’s character, Larroquette declined the offer.

He even declined an Emmy nomination in 1989, feeling that he had already been recognized enough for his work on the show.

Despite the passing of time, John Larroquette remains synonymous with the character of Dan Fielding.

Fans of Night Court were excited to see him reprise his role in the 2023 continuation of the series, proving that his comedic talents are just as sharp as ever.
