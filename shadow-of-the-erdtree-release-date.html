Shadow Of the Erdtree Theories

The image in the tweet looks to be Miquella, Malenia’s twin brother, riding Torrent across a field, according to this fantastic Reddit post. The field doesn’t like the ones from The Lands Between, suggesting that the events in Shadow of the Erdtree took place either in the past or in a completely different universe. Malenia is one of the most difficult Elden Ring monsters you can face in the open-world game, but you never get to see Miquella in his original form.

FromSoftware is hardly a newcomer to the idea of exploring the past. Players have transported hundreds of years into the past to battle Manus, Father of the Abyss, in the first and only Dark Souls DLC, Artorias of the Abyss. Whilst we haven’t witnessed time travel in Elden Ring, that doesn’t guarantee we won’t in the future.

There’s a potential that Miquella’s dream will be where the expansion occurs. This has previously occurred in Fia’s questline, one of the best Elden Ring quests, where you had the chance to battle a strong dragon in Fia’s dream. We are aware that Miquella is dozing off, and we may need to penetrate his head in order to find this other world.

In Elden Ring Shadow of the Erdtree, who is Miquella?

Miquella and Malenia are unique creatures known as Empyreans, and they have the potential to become gods. Despite the fact that they are both cursed by birth, their ailments are not the same. Malenia was afflicted with Scarlet Rot, which made her decay internally. The inability to mature, which keeps him imprisoned in a child’s body, is Miquella’s curse.

Miquella planted his own Haligtree in the Consecrated Snowfield, away from prying eyes so that it may thrive, to counteract the effects of their curses. You can see Miquella’s enormous arm as it hangs outside of the cocoon he made to shelter himself while the Haligtree grew during the boss battle with Mohg, Lord of Blood.

In an effort to declare themselves deities and increase their authority, Mohg abducted Miquella from the Haligtree. When the player beats Mohg in Mohgwyn Palace and leaves Miquella’s cocoon behind, Mohg’s strategy finally fails.

Nevertheless, without Miquella’s cocoon to keep the tree alive, the Haligtree has been left to rot. The tree is now dead and lifeless, with soldiers standing guard over what is left of it. Melania waits for her brother to come home at the base of the tree, but he is still unconscious inside Mohgwyn Palace.
