Ludwig Ahgren Wiki

Ludwig Ahgren is a live streamer, YouTuber, podcaster, esports commentator, and competitor from the United States. Ahgren is well known for his live streaming on Twitch from 2018 until late 2021, and on YouTube beginning in late 2021, where he broadcasts both video game-related and non-video game-related entertainment such as game shows, contests, and gambling. He is also well-known as an esports commentator at several Super Smash Bros. Melee competitions. Ahgren had been one of Twitch's most popular broadcasters since he began broadcasting full-time on February 16, 2019.

Ludwig Ahgren Early Life/Career

Early Life: - Ahgren grew up in a bilingual French-American family in Hollis, New Hampshire, with his mother and sister, his father passed away when he was ten years old. He graduated from Hollis/Brookline High School in New Hampshire in 2013. In 2012, he starred as Warner in his high school's performance of Legally Blonde. During this time, he launched "The Zany Side kick," his first step into video creation, with a Call of Duty commentary YouTube channel.

Professional Career: - Ahgren has had little but noticeable success as a Super Smash Bros. Melee competitor. In 2017, Ahgren won a series of games against two players ranked in the top 104 in Melee's community, Team Liquid's Chillindude, who was playing his tertiary Mario, and Tempo Storm's Vro. Ahgren also won a game against Weston Dennis, the world's 12th-ranked player at the time, but lost the set. Ahgren has had far greater success as a Melee commentator, having called matches at numerous major Melee tournaments, such as the GENESIS series. In a rematch in 2019, Chillindude defeated Ahgren at Mainstage, a major event in Ontario, California.

During 2018 and 2019, Ahgren's stream had a relatively tiny following, with the majority of his material being on Super Smash Bros. Melee, Mario Party 2, and Dark Souls gaming. His streams' popularity and subscriber count grew steadily over time. His viewership began to climb in July 2019 and surged drastically in October 2019.
