Updated college football bowl projections: TCU in Fiesta, Ohio State in Peach

May 2024 · 5 minute read

Read The Athletic’s latest college football bowl projections. 

Updated: Sunday at 2:30 a.m. ET.

TCU fell inches shy of the goal line and a Big 12 title in a 31-28 overtime loss to Kansas State, but the Horned Frogs (12-1) still are projected to qualify for the College Football Playoff. The only question remaining is whether TCU will remain at No. 3 or fall to No. 4 behind Ohio State in the final rankings

Advertisement

Kansas State (10-3) will play in the Sugar Bowl, likely against Alabama. Georgia (13-0) will surely be the No. 1 seed after routing LSU in Atlanta. The Dawgs will be back there for the Peach Bowl semifinal on Dec. 31. Michigan (13-0) as the No. 2 seed will head to the Fiesta Bowl.

Clemson (11-2) beat North Carolina in the ACC Championship Game and will head to the Orange Bowl, where its likely opponent is Tennessee.

Meanwhile, Tulane beat UCF on Saturday to win the AAC championship. In doing so it will earn the Group of 5’s New Year’s Six bowl berth, which this year is in the Cotton Bowl. Fresno State beat Boise State in the Mountain West title game and will play in the L.A. Bowl.

As for two schools ending long bowl droughts: Kansas looks likely to reach the Liberty Bowl in Memphis, while Connecticut should go to one of ESPN Events’ Florida bowls. We have the Huskies in Boca Raton.

Reminders:

  • Most bowls do not have to select in strict order of conference standings. “Big 12 No. 3” or “Big Ten No. 3” means third choice, not third place, which is why a team with a worse record or head-to-head loss can jump a conference rival.
  • The SEC office plays matchmaker with its bowls and schools.
  • ESPN Events owns and operates 16 of the 41 FBS bowls. Most of those bowls involve Group of 5 conferences. ESPN Events can move teams to create intriguing matchups.
  • All times Eastern.
