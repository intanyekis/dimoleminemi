Carrie Underwood is an American singer, songwriter, record producer, businesswoman, and actress who has established herself as the sixth highest-certified female artist, the second highest-certified country artist, and the highest-certified female country artist of all time on the RIAA‘s “Top Artists (Digital Singles)” ranking. Carrie initially gained popularity by winning the 4th season of American Idol in 2005 and has since released several popular albums which include Some Hearts, Play On, Blown Away, Storyteller, Cry Pretty, and Carnival Ride. Over the years, the singer has received at least 7 Grammy Awards, 11 Billboard Music Awards, and 15 American Music Awards.
